Step 3 of 9 — DALRRD registration

Are you registered with DALRRD as a Food Business Operator?

An FBO code is your first credential in the SA export system. PPECB cannot inspect or certify your products without it, and GACC/CIFER registration requires it as a mandatory identifier. Register at fbo.daff.gov.za — processing takes 5 to 15 business days.
Step 4 of 9 — Production unit codes

Do you have a Production Unit Code or Pack House Code?

Your PUC identifies your farm. Your PHC identifies your packhouse. Both appear on all export documentation and are required for PPECB inspection scheduling and GACC dossier preparation. Apply through DALRRD Directorate Food Safety and Quality Assurance.
Step 5 of 9 — PPECB certification

Is your farm or packhouse PPECB-certified?

PPECB issues three export passports required for every SA agricultural shipment to China: an export certificate, a food safety certificate (SA-GAP), and a pre-cooling certificate. Without all three, your goods cannot legally leave South Africa. PPECB also manages cold chain audits and laboratory MRL testing.
Step 6 of 9 — Digital certification

Are you registered on DALRRD's eCert platform?

eCert is DALRRD's digital phytosanitary certificate system. China requires phytosanitary certificates for all agricultural imports. Manual paper applications to China are being discontinued — eCert registration is now effectively mandatory. Register at ecert.dalrrd.gov.za.
Step 7 of 9 — Cold chain

Do you have verified cold chain infrastructure?

PPECB audits your packhouse and cold storage as part of certification. Chinese ports require temperature-controlled containers with 24-hour monitoring logs throughout the 30 to 40 day sea journey. Vessel captains submit daily temperature reports. Without a verified cold chain, your consignment risks port rejection on arrival.
Step 8 of 9 — Decree 280 labelling

Does your packaging include Simplified Chinese labelling?

Decree 280 (effective 1 June 2026) requires all food imports to carry mandatory fields in Simplified Chinese: legal product name, GACC registration number, storage temperature, batch codes, and a People's Republic of China destination declaration. Non-compliant labels result in port detention and potential destruction of the consignment.
